# Executive Summary
## ICO Standards Framework: Digital Product Passport for Cultural & Quality Goods (DPP-CQ)

**International Communication Organization (ICO)**
**July 2026 · Draft for Public Comment**
**Feedback:** info@icoun.org

---

### The Opportunity

Global trade is undergoing a profound shift — from *physical flow* to *trusted flow*. Digital Product Passports (DPPs) are emerging as the infrastructure for this new era, yet current standards address only a fraction of the picture:

- **Existing DPP frameworks**, led by advanced economies, focus primarily on industrial products and environmental data (e.g., EU ESPR carbon passports).
- **Cultural goods, geographical indication (GI) products, and intangible cultural heritage (ICH)** — categories representing hundreds of billions in global trade — lack dedicated digital trust standards.
- **Developing economies** face significant barriers in digital trust infrastructure: technology gaps, standard exclusion, and capacity constraints.

According to OECD/EUIPO (2025), global trade in counterfeit and pirated goods amounts to $467 billion annually, representing 2.3% of world imports. The UNESCO *Convention for the Safeguarding of the Intangible Cultural Heritage* recognizes digital technology as critical for cultural preservation — yet no comprehensive dedicated standard exists for cultural and quality goods.

### Our Vision

ICO is building a comprehensive **Digital Product Passport for Cultural & Quality Goods (DPP-CQ)** — an open, neutral, and inclusive digital trust standard that gives every product bearing cultural value and quality commitment a verifiable, traceable, and mutually recognizable digital identity.

**By 2030: 100,000+ products with global DPP-CQ identities, empowering 5,000+ SMEs, across 20+ countries and regions.**

### What is DPP-CQ?

DPP-CQ (ICO Std 2001) is a digital identity standard for cultural products, specialty agricultural goods, GI products, and ICH handicrafts. It defines:

- **Six data modules**: Base Identity (DID), Quality Data, Cultural Data, Geographical Indication, Traceability, and Credential Proof
- **Dual physical verification**: QR code + NFC, adapting to different cost scenarios
- **Three-level verification**: Quick check (<1s), Deep verification, Judicial-grade verification
- **ZKP-enabled privacy**: Prove compliance without disclosing core craft recipes, supplier identities, or commercial pricing
- **Mixed storage architecture**: Raw data stored off-chain (privacy-first), hash anchors on-chain (immutability)

### Why Now?

The technology is ready:
- **W3C DID Core** (Recommendation, 2022) and **Verifiable Credentials** provide a mature decentralized identity foundation
- **Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs)** have moved from labs to production-ready implementations
- **NFC and QR code** costs have dropped to commodity levels
- **Multi-chain architecture** has become industry consensus, avoiding vendor lock-in

The policy window is open:
- UN *Global Digital Compact* calls for inclusive digital infrastructure for developing nations
- UNECE's Global DPP Interoperability Initiative seeks broader participation
- UNESCO and WIPO both emphasize digital protection of cultural and GI assets

### Governance Model

ICO employs a **multi-stakeholder governance** model — the gold standard for international standards bodies (W3C, IETF, ISO):

- **General Assembly**: Supreme decision-making body, meets annually
- **Standards Council**: 25% government / 25% industry / 20% academia / 20% civil society / 10% Technical Secretariat (non-voting)
- **Technical Secretariat**: Neutral execution body — maintains reference implementations, operates the Root Resolver Network, does NOT vote on standards
- **Working Groups**: Open participation for all members

### Root Resolution Infrastructure

To ensure global cross-border verifiability, ICO operates:
- **ICO Root Resolver Network**: Global distributed DID resolution infrastructure
- **Tianji Credibility Registry (TCR)**: Unified credential hash registration and anchoring database

All DPP-CQ credentials register their DID and hash at root nodes — ensuring global traceability and judicial-grade verification. The architecture employs multi-region redundancy, 99.99% availability targets, and annual third-party security audits.

### Compatibility & Interoperability

ICO standards are built *on top of* — not in competition with — existing international standards:

| Standard | Relationship |
|---|---|
| **W3C DID/VC** | Core technology foundation, fully compliant |
| **GS1 / EPCIS** | Mapping and interoperability support |
| **UNECE DPP Initiative** | Active participant in global interoperability framework |
| **EU DPP / ESPR** | Data mapping for cross-system information sharing |
| **ISO 22000 / 26000** | Compatible with quality and social responsibility standards |

### Open Source & Accessibility

- **Standards documentation**: CC BY 4.0 — free to use, remix, adapt
- **Reference implementations**: Apache 2.0 — commercial-friendly, fully open
- **Multi-language SDKs**: Community-driven development
- **Lightweight options**: QR-code-only paths for low-resource environments
- **SME-friendly**: SaaS onboarding, no technical team required
